Case Study

Resistance Analysis in Acinetobacter spp.

An in-depth look at Prof. Dr. Eduardo Medeiros' project and the importance of genomics in combating superbugs.

By Felipe Alberto Lei • October 10, 2025

The Clinical Challenge of Acinetobacter

The genus Acinetobacter, especially Acinetobacter baumannii, represents one of the greatest challenges in modern medicine. Known for its incredible ability to acquire resistance to multiple antibiotics, it is one of the main causes of severe hospital-acquired infections, such as pneumonia and bloodstream infections. Understanding how this resistance emerges and spreads is crucial for the development of new treatment and control strategies.

The Project and Its Objectives

The project conducted by Prof. Dr. Eduardo Medeiros focused on investigating the genetic mechanisms that confer multidrug resistance in clinical isolates of Acinetobacter. The main objectives were:

  • Sequence the complete genome (WGS) of various strains isolated from patients.
  • Identify antibiotic resistance genes, virulence factors, and mobile genetic elements (such as plasmids and transposons).
  • Analyze the phylogenetic relationship among strains to understand clonal dissemination in the hospital environment.
  • Correlate genomic findings with antimicrobial susceptibility profiles (phenotype).

Project Infographic

To communicate the complex results clearly and visually, an infographic was developed that summarizes the workflow and main findings of the study. It illustrates everything from sample collection to bioinformatic analysis and identification of resistance mechanisms.

Conclusion and Impact

The results of this project provided valuable insights into the molecular epidemiology of Acinetobacter, highlighting the importance of genomic surveillance to monitor the emergence and dissemination of high-risk clones. The combination of laboratory techniques with robust bioinformatics analyses is fundamental to facing the growing threat of superbugs.
